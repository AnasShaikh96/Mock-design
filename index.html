How we work?

  • Surveying and Estimating Budget.

  • Preparation of Tender, Invitation of Bids and Finalization of Contractor.

  • Execution of work.

  • Finalisation of work.

  • Stage I : How do we carry out survey and prepare budget estimate?

    • We determine the importance of various scope aspects, such as structural repairs and aesthetics, through thorough internal and external surveys.
    • We draft a comprehensive budget estimate that breaks down overall and category-specific costs.
    • Our aim is to facilitate funding acquisition from various sources like sinking funds and repair funds to ensure a smooth project.
    • We prioritize tasks based on financial constraints using our detailed budgeted estimate.
    • Our focus remains on surveying for precise budgeting in repair and rehab projects.
    • We collaborate closely with senior surveyors and engineers for accurate preliminary surveys.
    • Rest assured, we maintain consistency in quantities and costs throughout your project.
    • Our team coordinates with site engineers and supervisors to guarantee top-quality work within budget constraints.

  • Stage II : Preparation of tender, invitation of bids and finalization of contractor.

  • Stage-III : Execution of Work.

    A three-tier system has been devised to achieve the best-desired results during the execution stage, once the contractor is finalized. To execute a job after thorough screening as detailed out above, the day-to-day functioning including checking various mixes being used, keeping stock of the material received, testing the same batch wise, liasoning between the members and the contractors etc. is taken care of by a full time qualified site engineer. The billing part is taken care of by the billing engineers who are responsible for the joint measurements, scrutiny of bill and preparation of payment certificate. The Project Engineer visits the site at suitable intervals depending upon site conditions and attends weekly or fortnightly meetings to review the progress of work along with the contractor and Representatives of clients and taking it to final completion.

  • Stage-IV : Finalisation of Work.

    In the final stages of a project, several crucial administrative tasks come into play. First, the process involves collecting the Performa Warranty, which outlines the terms and conditions of product or service guarantees. Additionally, obtaining the Work Completion Certificate is essential, as it signifies the successful conclusion of the project and adherence to agreed-upon specifications. To ensure longevity, collecting the Warranty Certificate from the paint company is vital, validating the quality and durability of applied coatings. Lastly, the collection of the Retention Revert Letter marks the release of withheld funds, cementing the financial closure of the project. These steps collectively ensure the project's success and provide necessary documentation for future reference.
